Key scientific facts
🧬
Strain: Andes virus — the only known hantavirus with documented limited human-to-human transmission. Normally transmitted via rodent urine, feces, or saliva.
⏱
Incubation: 1–8 weeks after exposure. Symptoms include fever, gastrointestinal illness, rapid progression to pneumonia and acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS).
🔬
Treatment: No approved antiviral and no licensed vaccine exists anywhere in the world. Early ICU admission and supportive care are the only evidence-based interventions.
📍
Source hypothesis: Index case likely contracted the virus birdwatching near rodent-heavy sites in Ushuaia, Argentina during a 4-month road trip through Chile, Uruguay, and Argentina before boarding.
📡
Transmission risk: Human-to-human spread documented only in very close prolonged contact. Attack rate among 160+ contacts is ~6% — low compared to airborne pathogens. WHO, CDC, and CIDRAP all assess public risk as very low. No cases identified outside MV Hondius passengers.
🌎
Monitoring period: Passengers are being monitored for 42 days from disembarkation (May 11), running through ~June 22. Illinois is separately investigating an unrelated hantavirus case in a resident exposed to rodent droppings at home — not linked to MV Hondius.
